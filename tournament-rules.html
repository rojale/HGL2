Tournament Rules

General Rules

Administration

Admins control the tournament and if they ask for proof, explanations, etc. you must comply with their requests/instructions or risk disqualification. Admin rulings are final and indisputable unless proper proof is supplied to overrule.

Discord Server

All players are required to join and be present in our Discord Server before and throughout the duration of every tournament.

Servers and Accounts

Players living in regions other than the tournament's designated region are allowed to play in the tournament, they just need to have an account on that tournament's server.

Players must fulfill all the tournament requirement and must have an active and valid battle.net account on the tournaments designated server. If a player does not fulfill all the tournament requirements or does not have a valid/active battle.net account linked they will be disqualified

Sign-up and Check-in

To sign up for a tournament you must find the tournament you wish to play in on our tournament page, click on the link, and then join the tournament through entering your battle.net tag and classes. (Players can leave at anytime leading up to the tournament).

To check in to a tournament you must go to the tournament you have signed up for and click the check-in button within the first hour leading up to the tournament..

Brackets and Seeding

Once a tournament starts a bracket will be generated with randomly seeded players and matches. (Players must check in to be seeded).

Tournament Format

3-Hero-1-Ban Conquest Format

Each player must submit three unique classes prior to the event that they will use for the entirety of the event.

Before every match both players will be required to ban 1 of their opponent's classes that they will NOT be allowed to use during the match.

When a player wins a game, the class used by the winning player cannot be use for the remainder of the match.

The losing player can keep the same class or choose to change to their other class.

Players will be told what classes their opponent has available, but they won't know which class is picked until both players have picked.

Players may edit their decks at any point in the tournament as long as they DO NOT leave the challenge.

Players can only have 1 deck prepared for each of their 3 chosen classes. (You can ask your opponent for a screenshot of their deckslots WITHIN the challenge.)

Matches will be played in a best of 3 format. The first player to win 2 games is considered the winner of the match and advances.

A player must win one game with each of their 2 non-ban classes to win the match.

Tournament Process

Friend Requests and Challenges

To send a friend request open your Hearthstone client, click on friends, click 'Add,' type in your opponent's battle.net tag, which you can find on the Battlefy match page, and hit enter.

To send a challenge open your Hearthstone client, click on friends, find your opponent's battle.net tag, then click the 'Challenge' button next to his name, and accept the challenge.

When a player's match is posted they have 10 minutes to send a friend request and challenge them or they will get disqualified.

Reporting

To report a game in a tournament you must open your match on the bracket, click report score, and update the game count.

Players are required to report their matches on time, correctly, and truthfully or they will get disqualified.

Players are required to take a screenshot of all their game wins or they will get disqualified.

Disputes

If a player's match becomes disputed and their opponent has incorrectly reported a win they are required to immediately contact an admin and supply them with screenshots of their game wins. (Lack of screenshots will result in a disqualification)

Disconnects

When a player disconnects before a game is played or between games they have 5 minutes to re-challenge or they are disqualified.

When a player disconnects from a game it is their responsibility to re-join the game and play it out from that point or they will be given a lose.

If a player disconnects from a game but can provide a screenshot showing that they had imminent lethal they will be given a win.

Player Conduct and Behavior

All players must be fair and have good sportsmanship when playing in any of our tournaments.

If anybody intentionally disrespects the sense of fair play and sportsmanship they will be disqualified from the tournament and banned from our chat and all future tournaments.

Toxicity, profanity, and inappropriate behavior will not be tolerated and will result in a permanent ban.

Extended-Miscellaneous Rules

In the event of a tie/draw game where both participants drop to 0 health points in the same turn (both participants will see a "Defeat" as the end screen) the game will be remade with both participants using the very same deck again.

If you pick the wrong class, you must concede within the first two turns or you get disqualified. If your opponent picks the wrong class and you do not get proof of them playing it out and/or you play the match to the end the game will count and the player will not get disqualified.

Spectators are allowed, however spectators are not allowed to spectate both players simultaneously.

Players are allowed to stream/record their matches throughout the tournament, however, we are not responsible for any cheating, disconnects, or anything else that happens as a result of you streaming the tournament.
